Ways to get involved

There are many ways to be part of Nottingham Growing Network.

Volunteer at a Garden
Join one of our 35 gardens as a volunteer. Help grow food and be part of a welcoming community. No experience needed.
Join a Lunch Club
Come along to one of our community lunches where fresh garden produce is cooked and shared. Free and open to all.
Bring the Kids
Many gardens run children's environmental clubs and family activities. A great way to connect young people with nature.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about getting involved with NGN.

Do I need gardening experience to volunteer? ▼
Not at all! All of our community gardens welcome complete beginners. You will learn as you go alongside friendly and experienced growers. Just bring enthusiasm and wear clothes you don't mind getting dirty.
Is there a cost to get involved? ▼
Almost everything across the NGN network is completely free. Volunteering sessions, community lunches, kids clubs and many workshops are free to attend. Some specialised courses may have a small cost — check with your local garden.
How do I find my nearest garden? ▼
Visit our Gardens page where you can view an interactive map of all 35 community gardens across Nottingham. Click any pin to see the garden name, address and contact email. You can also search by name, area or postcode.
Can children get involved? ▼
Yes! Many of our gardens run children's environmental clubs, family activities and kids cooking sessions. Activities and age ranges vary by garden — contact your local garden to find out what they offer for young people.
I run a community garden — can I join the network? ▼
We would love to hear from you! Get in touch with us using the contact form or by emailing Barbara directly. NGN supports community gardens across Nottingham with resources, guidance and advocacy.
How is food from the gardens distributed? ▼
Food grown in our community gardens is shared with volunteers who help tend the gardens, donated to local food banks, used in social eating events and offered to the wider community for small donations. Nothing goes to waste!
