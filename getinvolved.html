Get Involved

Whether you want to volunteer, join a garden, or connect with NGN — here's how to get started.

Ways to get involved

There are many ways to be part of Nottingham Growing Network — find the one that suits you.

🌱
Volunteer at a Garden
Join one of our 35 community gardens as a volunteer. Help grow food, tend the garden, and be part of a welcoming community. No experience needed.
🍽️
Join a Lunch Club
Come along to one of our regular community lunches where fresh garden produce is cooked and shared. Free and open to all.
📚
Attend a Workshop
Join a Green Skills course, seed swap, composting workshop or cooking session. Check our events page for upcoming sessions.
👶
Bring the Kids
Many of our gardens run children's environmental clubs and family activities. A great way to get young people connected with nature and food growing.
🤝
Support a Garden
Help support a local community garden through donations, skills sharing, or helping with fundraising and grant applications.
📣
Spread the Word
Follow NGN on social media and share our updates with your community. Help more Nottingham residents discover their local community garden.

How to get started

Getting involved is simple — just follow these steps.

1
Find your nearest garden
Use our interactive map on the Gardens page to find the community garden closest to you. Each pin shows the garden name, address and contact email.
2
Contact the garden directly
Each community garden is independent and manages its own activities. Contact them directly using the email address on the map to find out when they meet, what they offer and how to get involved.
3
Turn up and get growing
Most gardens welcome drop-in volunteers during their regular sessions. Just turn up, introduce yourself, and get stuck in. No experience or specialist knowledge needed — just enthusiasm!
4
Check our events page
Keep an eye on our Events page for network-wide events, workshops, seed swaps and community lunches happening across Nottingham.
