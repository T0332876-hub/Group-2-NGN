## Current Social Media Situation

- Facebook: Exists but unmanageable - predominantly used by domestic gardeners
- Instagram: Exists but never used
- No coherent social media strategy

## Recommendation

### Facebook
Rename the existing page and leave it as a community-led space.
This reduces the management burden while maintaining community engagement.

### Instagram
Develop this as the main controlled communication channel for NGN.
More visual platform suited to showcasing gardens, events and produce.

---

## Instagram Content Strategy

### Post Types
- Garden of the Week: Highlight one garden with address and contact details
- Event posts: Upcoming workshops, volunteering sessions, lunch clubs
- Network updates: Achievements, collaborations, funding news
- Informational posts: How to get involved, what NGN does

### Posting Schedule
- Minimum 3 posts per week
- Regular stories for real-time updates and event reminders
- Reference the interactive garden map in posts to drive website traffic

## Website Design Decisions

### Colour Scheme
- Primary: Dark green (#3B6D11)
- Secondary: Mid green (#639922)
- Light: Pale green (#EAF3DE)
- Text: Near black (#1a1a1a)
- Rationale: Green is widely associated with nature, growth and sustainability
  making it appropriate for a community gardening organisation (Upward Arrow, 2025)

### Typography
- Font: Nunito Sans (sans-serif)
- Headings: 24-32px
- Subheadings: 18-22px
- Body text: 14-16px
- Line height: 1.5 minimum
- Rationale: Nunito Sans is known for its clean modern appearance and is
  ideal for web design and digital interfaces (Rahaman, 2025)

### Visual Design
- High quality photos from Barbara used throughout
- Clean white space between sections
- Card-based layout for activities and events
- Mobile responsive design
