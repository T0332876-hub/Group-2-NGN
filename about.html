About Nottingham Growing Network

A long-standing community initiative connecting 35 food growing groups across Nottingham.

Who we are

Nottingham Growing Network is a long-standing initiative that builds links between 35 community food growing groups in Nottingham, with a focus on healthy eating, wellbeing, sustainable gardening and biodiversity, offering support, guidance, and advocacy.

We are a newly constituted network, now registered with Companies House. Our community gardens teach people how to grow food and cook with fresh ingredients, making affordable meals accessible for everyone.

Food grown is shared with volunteers and local food banks, used in our social eating projects, and offered to the wider community. Each garden is unique and responds to its own local community's needs.

What our gardens offer

Activities vary across our 35 gardens — each garden responds to its own community.

Community Food Growing
Growing food together and sharing it with volunteers, food banks and local people.
Volunteering Opportunities
Regular volunteering sessions — no experience needed, just enthusiasm.
Environmental Kids Clubs
Engaging young people with nature, food growing and outdoor activities.
Lunch Clubs & Social Eating
Regular community lunches cooked using fresh garden produce, open to all.
Outdoor Yoga & Wellbeing
Wellbeing activities in green spaces across Nottingham's community gardens.
Green Skills Courses
Workshops on composting, seed saving, natural pest control and sustainable growing.

Who we support

Our gardens are open to everyone, with a particular focus on those who need us most.

Families in Poverty
Families with young children, giving access to fresh food, outdoor activities and community connection.
Older People
Older people living alone, providing community connection, social activities and outdoor time.
Mental Health Support
People with complex mental health issues and learning difficulties supported through nature-based activities.
